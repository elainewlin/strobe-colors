Assessing color addition and subtraction with strobes

INSERT PHOTO OF THE ACTUAL DEVICE We flash red, green, and blue lights at a piddler, an oscillating pump. The pump produces a small pulse of liquid at a rate of 60 pulses per second. The rates of the lights affect the motion of the droplets. For any color,
  • slower than piddler → droplet moving down
  • faster than piddler → droplet moving up
  • same as piddler → droplet stays still
Colors can be created through additive mixing and subtractive mixing. Each process works by combining differing amounts of three "primary colors" to create the desired hue.
